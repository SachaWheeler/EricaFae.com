WRITING/DIRECTING ORIGINAL WORK

Fae’s original work as writer/director/performer brings stories of powerful, radical women from history onto the stage and screen. FILM: Fae’s first feature film, To Keep The Light, premiered this spring to rave reviews, and thus far on its festival circuit has won Best Feature Cinematography at LVFF.  Her short films have screened at festivals worldwide, at the SOHO House’s Best of New Filmmakers Series, and she was awarded Best Short at the Salento International Film Festival and Best Actress at the BAC Film Festival at the Brooklyn Museum. THEATRE: Her play, Take What Is Yours, co-written with Jill A. Samuels - was a Critics’ Pick in both the New York Times & Backstage. Take What Is Yours was developed in residencies at New Georges and IRT, read at New York Theatre Workshop’s “Readings at 3” series, shown in workshop at the New OHIO Theatre, and premiered Off-Broadway at 59E59 Theaters.  Jill & Erica are currently at work on both a mini-series and a new theatre project.  Fae’s solo play, A Girl Joan,  based on the imagined heart-life of Joan of Arc, was commissioned by Dance Theater Workshop and also performed at The Culture Project, NYTW’s Just Add Water Festival, The Present Company and HERE.  Other original theatre includes Dreaming de Pizan (developed in residency at HERE’s HARP program), Until Now (toured to Edinburgh Fringe), and numerous collaborative works.


ACTING

FILM/TV: Recurring roles on HBO’s Boardwalk Empire (Season 5) and Doll & Em (Season 2). Other credits include: Synecdoche New York, The Savages, Little Children, Please Give, Baby Mama, The Music Never Stops, East of Acadia, Futurestates.tv episode Hollow, and Law & Order.  She also worked for a month as a rehearsal actress with Philip Seymour Hoffman and Charlie Kaufman on Synecdoche New York.  STAGE: As well as performing in her original work (below), credits include work with Martha Clarke (Vienna Lusthaus; Revisited, A Midsummer Night’s Dream, and Hans Christian Andersen collectively at NYTW, The Kennedy Center, A.R.T., A.C.T., The Walker Arts Center), V. Gautier-Martin (Genoa 01/developed at Robert Wilson’s Watermill Center), Ripe Time (Fire Throws/Antigone, played Antigone), Juggernaut (Lapis Blue Blood Red, playing Artemisia Gentileschi), Ping Chong’s American Gothic, and many others. 


TEACHING/COACHING

Fae teaches Grotowski-based physical training and Viewpoints at the Yale School of Drama and The New School for Drama (MFA programs).  She has also taught at NYU/Tisch’s Experimental Theatre Wing (where she studied), Master Classes for the SITI Company & Princeton, and co-teaches an annual summer intensive in Grotowski-based training.  She was Movement Coach/Director on Hamlet at Yale Rep (dir. James Bundy), The Golden Dragon for The Play Company (dir. Ed Iskandar), and 2 seasons for Hudson Valley Shakespeare Festival.
